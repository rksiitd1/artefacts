DNA Extraction Flow Diagram

1. 0.5 g soil in Bio-101 Multimix 2 Matrix Tube (Qbiogene, Heidelberg, Germany)
↓
2. Add 0.5 ml CTAB extraction buffer (see section 2.2.1) and 0.5 ml phenol-chloroform-isoamylalcohol (25:24:1) at pH 8.0
↓
3. Lyse sample for 30 sec at 16,000 x g in a homogeniser
↓
4. Separate aqueous phase by centrifugation at 16,000 x g for 5 min at 4°C
↓
5. Extract aqueous phase and remove phenol by mixing with an equal volume of chloroform – isoamylalcohol (24:1)
↓
6. Repeat centrifugation at 16,000 x g for 5 min at 4°C
↓
7. Precipitate total nucleic acids from extracted aqueous layer with two volumes of 30% polyethylene glycol - 1.6 M NaCl (see section 2.2.3) for 2 h at room temperature followed by centrifugation at 4°C for 10 min
↓
8. Wash pelleted nucleic acid in ice cold 70% ethanol
↓
9. Air dry
↓
10. Resuspend in 50 μl of RNase free Tris EDTA buffer (pH 7.4)

Flow diagram for extraction of nucleic acids from soil according to Griffiths et al.'s (2000) method of co-extraction of DNA and RNA.
